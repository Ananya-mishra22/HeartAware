Recognizing Cardiac Arrest

Cardiac arrest is a sudden, unexpected loss of heart function, breathing, and consciousness. Unlike a heart attack, which occurs when blood flow to a part of the heart is blocked, cardiac arrest is caused by an electrical malfunction in the heart that disrupts its pumping action. This disruption stops blood flow to the brain, lungs, and other organs.

Immediate recognition and action are crucial for survival. Every minute without treatment decreases the chance of survival by 7-10%.
